Monica Lestari Paramita

Lecturer in Data Science, Information School, University of Sheffield

I am a researcher in Information Retrieval. My research focuses on the study of bias and transparency in information retrieval and multilingual information access. I am especially interested in investigating how search engines should be designed to improve user awareness of biases in search engine results, and evaluating how users interact with such systems. I am also interested in researching multilingual information access and bridging knowledge gap across contents in different languages.

Research

I have worked in various areas in Information Retrieval and Natural Language Processing over the past 17 years, specifically in issues related to bias and transparency in information access. I am also interested in researching cross-lingual similarity in Wikipedia; this includes creating methods to measure cross-lingual similarity, understanding why dissimilar information exists, how they impact different users (e.g., users in different locations or those speaking different languages) and how these issues should be mitigated.

I have co-authored over 30 publications, ranging from conference papers, book chapters, and journal articles.
